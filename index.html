Kodak Animation Concepts

Kodak Alaris is a company that is all about helping people capture and connect the emotional moments that defines our lives. Their imperative is finding the Kodak Moment in each day.

This is a site to test Kodak Splash Animation concepts in CSS3. It is also a way to explain interaction concepts through code. All graphics are made in CSS3. Lets get it.

1

This is the first iteration of the Kodak Splash loading screen.

CONCEPT

This interaction involves the animation of 3 loading dots fading in and out on screen. The animation is punctuated by the ease of the fade. Users are able to anticipate the screen loading time.

The animation is infinite and continues until the screen loads and takes the user into the Kodak feed.

Variations of this effect is possible and involves adjusting the timing of the dots as well as the position of the dots.

2

This is the second iteration of the Kodak Splash loading screen.

CONCEPT

The second animation concept involves bokeh objects floating around the screen.

I have shown the animation to have a kinetic effect reacting to the other bokeh as they touch. Upon touching the bokeh will combine colors, bounce off each other, and change paths.

The floating bokeh acts as a loading screen and maintains constant speed throughout loading.

3

This is the third iteration of the Kodak Splash loading screen.

CONCEPT

The last interaction involves multiple bokeh circles fading out as the App is loading.

The animation only plays once and puts you directly into the Kodak App. The fluidity of the animation allows flexibility in the App's loading time.

Variations of this effect is also possible by reversing the animation.
